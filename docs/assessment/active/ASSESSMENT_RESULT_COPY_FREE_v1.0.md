# SoftFocus — Assessment Result Copy (Free Output)

Version: v1.0  
Status: ACTIVE  
Scope: User-facing core product output

---

## Purpose

Define the **exact role and tone** of the free assessment result.

The free result must:
- stand on its own as a product,
- deliver clarity without paywall pressure,
- create intellectual trust,
- justify future reassessment.

---

## Structure of Free Result

The free assessment output MUST contain exactly:

1. Burnout Stage (Label)
2. Primary Risk Vector
3. Interpretation Summary
4. Forward-Looking Signal

No additional sections are permitted.

---

## 1. Burnout Stage (Label)

Rules:
- single, stable label
- neutral language
- no emotional adjectives
- no medical terminology

Example format:
> **Current Stage: Sustained Load**

This label must be:
- repeatable across assessments,
- safe to quote or share,
- non-stigmatizing.

---

## 2. Primary Risk Vector

Purpose:
Identify **where degradation is most likely to occur next**.

Allowed vectors:
- Cognitive Load
- Decision Fatigue
- Recovery Inefficiency
- Attention Fragmentation
- Sustained Overextension

Format:
> **Primary Risk Vector: Decision Fatigue**

No scoring.  
No percentages.  
No alarm language.

---

## 3. Interpretation Summary

Tone:
- analytical
- observational
- non-prescriptive

Rules:
- explain *why* this state emerges,
- avoid advice,
- avoid responsibility framing.

Example:
> “Your responses indicate that decision quality is being maintained through increased effort rather than sustainable recovery.”

---

## 4. Forward-Looking Signal

Purpose:
Create a **reason to return**, not urgency.

Must include:
- conditional logic,
- time relevance,
- reassessment justification.

Example:
> “If this pattern persists for several more weeks, reassessment becomes meaningful to detect early degradation.”

---

## Explicit Exclusions

The free result MUST NOT include:
- recommendations
- recovery protocols
- urgency language
- emotional reassurance
- upsell copy

---

## Product Identity Rule

The free assessment result must feel:
> “Complete — but not finished.”
