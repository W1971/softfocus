## What This Page Is

This is not an academic bibliography.

It is a **map of professional domains**
that inform how SoftFocus thinks about burnout risk.

---

## Occupational Burnout Research

This field studies how long-term work conditions
affect performance sustainability.

Commonly discussed concepts:
- effort–recovery imbalance
- gradual exhaustion
- hidden compensation
- disengagement over time

Used widely in:
- organizational psychology
- workplace health research

---

## Human Factors & Cognitive Load

Human factors research focuses on how people perform
in complex, high-responsibility environments.

Commonly applied in:
- aviation
- healthcare
- engineering
- safety-critical operations

Key ideas:
- decision fatigue
- vigilance decline
- error probability under load

---

## Systems & Reliability Thinking

This field studies how failures emerge
in complex systems over time.

Used in:
- engineering
- operations
- safety management
- incident analysis

Key ideas:
- leading vs lagging indicators
- latent risk
- compensation masking problems

---

## Longitudinal Assessment Practices

Longitudinal assessment focuses on change over time,
not single-point measurement.

Used in:
- performance monitoring
- risk analysis
- quality assurance

Key ideas:
- trend detection
- meaningful reassessment intervals
- avoiding gamified scoring

---

## Why This Matters

SoftFocus combines these perspectives
to understand burnout as a **systemic risk**,
not a personal flaw or emotional weakness.
